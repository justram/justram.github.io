excerpt: "The Anserini IR toolkit has come a long way since efforts began in 2015. Although the goals of the project - to bridge research and practice in information retrieval, and to provide reproducible, easy-to-use baselines - have remained constant, the world has changed quite a bit. We discuss how Anserini has evolved in response to this changing environment, the most significant of which is the advent of transformer-based retrieval models that did not exist when the project started. The bi-encoder architecture provides a framework for understanding retrieval models based on dense and sparse vector representations, and offers a reference for conveying the capabilities of our toolkit. Anserini provides end-to-end first-stage retrieval based on single-vector learned dense and sparse representations, directly building on the open-source Lucene search library and the ONNX runtime.
